# Proposals and business case

Use when the user needs a written proposal, a statement of work or a cost-of-inaction case. Pull the offer, proof and price band from the profile and deal context from the CRM or `saleasy-pipeline`.

## Three-tier proposal
Offer Good, Better, Best rather than a single price. Anchor three named tiers, mark the middle "Recommended" and make the scope difference clear. Include an explicit out-of-scope section, a short implementation timeline (for example 90 days) and a three-touch proposal follow-up sequence.

## Findings-to-ROI
Turn each finding into a conservative value range, always labelled an estimate based on industry averages, never a promise. Revenue lift equals current volume times an expected improvement times customer value. Cost saved equals hours saved times loaded cost or tools and headcount avoided. Risk avoided equals probability times average cost. Present conservative, likely and optimistic columns with a rough break-even in months. The size of the gap, not the price of the product, justifies the investment.

## Rules
No invented metrics; if a number is unknown, ask or mark it an assumption. Factual, neutral language. Lead with the buyer's outcome, not a feature list.
