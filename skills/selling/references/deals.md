# Meetings, pipeline and forecast

## Call prep one-pager
Account snapshot (what they do, size, relevant recent news). Attendees (role, likely priorities, one note each). Deal state (stage, value, what was agreed last, the goal of this call). Agenda, 3 to 5 time-boxed points. Discovery questions built from a framework (SPIN or Gap Selling). Likely objections with honest answers backed by proof. The specific next-step commitment to ask for.

## Call summary
Decisions made. Action items with owner and due date. Risks and open questions. The agreed next step with a date. Drafts: a follow-up email to the prospect and a three-line internal CRM note.

## Pipeline review
Rank open deals: value, stage, days in stage, last activity, next step (or flag if missing), risk level. Score each against the chosen qualification framework so the gaps (no champion, no economic buyer, no critical event) become the risks. Then a prioritized weekly action list: deals to push, deals to rescue, deals to disqualify. Deals with no next step are the silent killers; call them out.

## Forecast
Categorize each deal: commit, best case, pipeline. Weight by stage or signals and state the method. Scenarios: worst (commit only), likely (commit plus weighted best case), best (all in play). Gap analysis: forecast versus target and what would close the gap. List assumptions plainly so the user can challenge them.
