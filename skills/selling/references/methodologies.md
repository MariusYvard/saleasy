# Sales methodologies

Lenses for discovery, qualification and deal review. Pick the one that fits the deal size and motion; do not force all of them onto a small deal. Map each to the offer and persona from the profile.

## Qualification
BANT: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline. Light and fast for high-volume motion; pair it with a real pain check. MEDDIC: Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Identify pain, Champion. For complex multi-stakeholder deals; use it to expose what is missing. SPICED: Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical event, Decision. Ties pain to a dated critical event, which creates urgency; pairs with the tension signal. POWERFUL: an eight-dimension scorecard rated 1 to 5 each (Pain, Opportunity-cost, Wants, Executive influence, Resources, Fear of failure, Unequivocal trust, Little things); the trust and little-things axes catch deals that look qualified but stall on confidence or neglected details.

## Discovery
SPIN: Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-payoff; sequence so the prospect articulates the cost before you propose. Challenger: Teach, Tailor, Take control; lead with a commercial insight that reframes their thinking. Gap Selling: map current state to desired future state; quantify the gap so value equals the gap, not the price.

## How to apply
Call prep: choose a discovery framework and draft the question list from it; choose a qualification framework and list what you still need to learn. Pipeline and forecast: score each deal against the chosen framework; missing elements are the risks to surface. Objections: separate a real objection from a reflex, acknowledge, ask to understand the root, then answer with proof. Never argue.
