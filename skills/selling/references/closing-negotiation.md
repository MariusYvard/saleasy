# Closing and negotiation

Knowing when to close, how to close, how to handle objections and how to negotiate without eroding value. Use the proof and pricing context from the profile.

## Close readiness score
Before any close attempt, check nine items: champion active, economic buyer met, budget confirmed, decision criteria addressed, a prospect-driven timeline, a mutual close plan, no open objections, a prospect-initiated next step, procurement scoped. 7 or more close now, 4 to 6 advance, under 4 requalify.

## Closing techniques
Match the technique to the signal: assumptive (strong signals, move to logistics), summary (recap value then ask), date-anchored (tie to their critical event), if-then (trade a concession for a commitment), alternative (two ways forward, both a yes), direct (just ask, when the relationship supports it).

## Closing conversation
Recap the problem, confirm the value, surface any remaining concern, ask clearly, then stop talking for ten to fifteen seconds. The silence is the point.

## Objections
Classify first: real (a genuine gap), condition (a true blocker like no budget), smokescreen (a deflection), delay (a stall). Use ACIR: acknowledge, clarify, isolate ("if we resolve this, is there anything else"), resolve with proof. The "just one more thing" late ask: acknowledge without agreeing, test if it is real, then trade, never simply give. Prevent objections by addressing the predictable ones in discovery and the proposal and inoculate against procurement.

## Negotiation
Know the zone of possible agreement before conceding. Use a tiered concession ladder and trade for every give. Tier 1 low cost: payment terms, a phased start, a small add-on, CSM access. Tier 2 moderate: extra seats, free onboarding. Tier 3 last resort: a capped discount (about 10 to 15 percent) tied to a logo, a case study or prepayment. For procurement, hit a target number without cutting total value: bundle, phase payment or relabel line items. Get procurement and any invisible stakeholder named early; they kill more deals than price.

## Stakeholder politics
Map each stakeholder on support level against influence. High-influence neutral: convert now. High-influence blocker: engage urgently and diagnose the cause (turf, budget, risk, a competing priority, already chose a competitor) and script to it. Low-influence supporter: turn into a referrer.

## Champion builder
Score the champion on access, credibility, pain alignment, motivation and activity. Run the personal-win exercise (what this deal does for them) and give an arming kit: a sixty-second internal pitch, a one-pager, an ROI snapshot, an objection-prep sheet. A quiet champion is a yellow flag.

## Deal gone dark
Send an honest check-in, not a guilt trip. Use a confidence temperature check ("on a scale of one to ten, where is this and what would make it a ten"). If still silent, try a second thread to another stakeholder, then ask permission to close the file. A clean walk-away often re-triggers a reply.
