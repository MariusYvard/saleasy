# Marketing playbooks

Detailed guidance per content job. Load only the section you need.

## LinkedIn post
Structure: hook line that names the buyer pain, 2 to 4 short body lines with one concrete idea or data point, one clear call to action. Scannable, short paragraphs, 0 to 3 relevant tags. Offer two angle variants and let the user pick before polishing. Hooks that work: a specific number, a counterintuitive claim tied to the persona, a short story opener, a direct question the buyer already asks. Never open with "In today's world". For LinkedIn, put links in the first comment and engage in the first sixty to ninety minutes after posting.

## LinkedIn profile
Headline: outcome for the buyer plus who it is for, not just a job title. About: the first two lines must stand alone (the fold); lead with who you help and how, then proof, then a call to action. Featured: pin the strongest proof asset from the profile.

## Editorial calendar
Output a table: date or slot, theme, format (post, article, carousel, video idea), funnel stage, hook. Align themes to the buyer journey: awareness, consideration, decision. Cadence matches realistic capacity, not a vanity number. 2 to 4 weeks per plan.

## Campaign
One page: objective and KPI, audience segment, channel mix, core message and proof, asset list with owners, timeline, success metric and review date. Tie the message to the differentiation from the profile. No invented budget.

## Long-form copy
Blog: title with search intent, scannable H2s, one idea per section, concrete examples, a call to action matched to funnel stage. Landing page: above-the-fold value proposition, proof, objection handling, one primary CTA repeated. Newsletter: one theme, a personal angle, one link out.

## SEO
Pick the intent (informational, commercial, transactional) before the keyword. On-page: title, meta, H1, structure, internal links, schema where relevant. Content gap: compare against the top ranking pages and the competitors from the profile, list what is missing. For a full technical or multi-page audit, fetch the live site and report by category (crawlability, indexability, Core Web Vitals, structured data). Do not fabricate metrics; measure or mark unknown.

## Repurposing
Turn one source piece into many. Map a pillar (blog, video, podcast, report) to platform derivatives: LinkedIn post, carousel, short clip, thread, newsletter section, quote graphic. Keep a pillar to supporting to micro hierarchy and batch the production.

## Lead magnets
Pick a hook formula that matches the audience: shortcut, template or swipe file, checklist, calculator, resource list, case study. Choose the format by speed-to-value, not effort to make. Validate on three tests: specific, fast to value, relevant to the buyer pain.

## Positioning
When the offer needs a sharper angle, pick a frame and state the case plainly: challenger reframe, category creation, niche domination, value-proposition canvas, competitive matrix, jobs-to-be-done, blue-ocean. Validate the angle: is it true, is it differentiated, does the buyer care, can you prove it. Feed a strong positioning line back into the profile so outreach and content stay consistent.
